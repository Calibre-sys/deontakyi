About

I studied petroleum engineering, but I kept drifting toward code. So I taught myself to build: models that make sense of messy data, web apps that let non-coders run machine learning, and small tools that take care of the boring work. The through-line isn't a field — it's liking problems and wanting to make things work a little better than they did before.

I graduated first in my class and I've published research, but mostly I'm someone who'll sit with a hard problem until it gives. Away from the screen: football, reading, and writing code for the fun of it.
